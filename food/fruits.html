Apples

Description & Importance:

Apples are a popular and healthy fruit enjoyed all over the world. They are rich in fiber and vitamin C, which help improve digestion and strengthen immunity. Eating apples regularly supports heart health and helps control blood sugar levels.

Tips

Eat with the peel

Choose fresh apples

Avoid sweetened apple products




Nutrition Facts(per 100 g) vitamins
Calories 52 kcal C
Protein 0.3 g Potassium
Fat 0.2 g
Carbohydrates 14 g

Bananas

Description & Importance:

Bananas are an excellent source of natural energy. They are rich in potassium, which helps maintain healthy muscles and nerves. Bananas are easy to eat and are perfect before or after exercise. They also support digestion and improve mood.

Tips

Eat before exercise

Avoid overripe bananas

Pair with nuts

Oranges

Description & Importance:

Oranges are well known for their high vitamin C content. They help boost the immune system and keep the body hydrated. Oranges also support skin health and protect the body from infections. Eating whole oranges is healthier than drinking orange juice.

Tips

Eat whole oranges instead of juice

Avoid added sugar

Consume fresh

Calories 47 kcal C
Protein 0.9 g Calcium
Fat 0.1 g Folate
Carbohydrates 12 g Magnesium

Strawberries

Description & Importance:

Strawberries are delicious and nutritious fruits rich in antioxidants and vitamin C. They help improve heart health and support the immune system. Strawberries are low in calories and are a great addition to a healthy diet when eaten fresh

Eat fresh

Healthy Food

Healthy food plays a vital role in maintaining both physical and mental well-being. It is not just about eating to satisfy hunger, but about nourishing the body with essential nutrients that help it function properly. A healthy diet provides the energy needed for daily activities, supports the immune system, improves concentration, and reduces the risk of chronic diseases. Eating healthy means consuming a balanced variety of foods that include fruits, vegetables, whole grains, lean proteins, and healthy fats. Fruits and vegetables are rich in vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants that protect the body from illness and strengthen immunity. Whole grains such as oats, brown rice, and whole wheat bread provide fiber, which aids digestion and helps maintain stable blood sugar levels.

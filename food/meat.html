Lean Beef (Grilled or Boiled)

Description & Importance:

Lean beef is an excellent source of high-quality protein and essential micronutrients. It is especially important for people who suffer from low energy levels or iron deficiency. Lean beef supports muscle strength, improves physical performance, and plays a vital role in red blood cell formation due to its high iron and vitamin B12 content.

Tips

Choose cuts labeled “lean” or “low fat”

Avoid frying; grilling or boiling is healthier

Consume in moderation (2–3 times per week)




Nutrition Facts(per 100 g) vitamins
B12
Calories 170–180 kcal Iron
Protein 26 g Zinc
Fat 7 g Phosphorus
Carbohydrates 0 g B6

Rabbit Meat

Description & Importance:

Rabbit meat is one of the leanest and healthiest types of meat. It is rich in protein while being very low in fat and cholesterol. This makes it a perfect choice for heart-healthy diets and weight-loss plans. Rabbit meat is also gentle on the digestive system.

Tips

Best cooked by boiling or baking

Avoid adding excess fats during cooking

Suitable for frequent consumption




Nutrition Facts(per 100 g) vitamins
Calories 135 kcal B12
Protein 29 g Potassium
Fat 3 g Selenium
Carbohydrates 0 g Phosphorus

Pigeon Meat (Skinless, Cooked)

Description & Importance:

Pigeon meat is a nutrient-dense protein source traditionally valued for its strength-boosting properties. It contains a good balance of protein and healthy fats and is particularly beneficial for individuals recovering from weakness or fatigue.

Tips

Always remove skin

Avoid stuffing to control calories

Consume occasionally




Nutrition Facts(per 100 g) vitamins
B12
Calories 190–200 kcal Iron
Protein 28 g Zinc
Fat 8-9 g Phosphorus
Carbohydrates 0 g Selenium

Duck Meat (Skinless)

Description & Importance:

Duck meat is richer in fat compared to other poultry, making it a high-energy food. While it provides valuable nutrients, it should be consumed in moderation, especially in calorie-controlled diets.

Tips

Always remove skin

Limit consumption in weight-loss diets

Best eaten occasionally




Nutrition Facts(per 100 g) vitamins
Calories 200-210 kcal B12
Protein 23 g B3
Fat 11-13 g Iron
Carbohydrates 0 g Zinc

Healthy Food

Healthy food plays a vital role in maintaining both physical and mental well-being. It is not just about eating to satisfy hunger, but about nourishing the body with essential nutrients that help it function properly. A healthy diet provides the energy needed for daily activities, supports the immune system, improves concentration, and reduces the risk of chronic diseases. Eating healthy means consuming a balanced variety of foods that include fruits, vegetables, whole grains, lean proteins, and healthy fats. Fruits and vegetables are rich in vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants that protect the body from illness and strengthen immunity. Whole grains such as oats, brown rice, and whole wheat bread provide fiber, which aids digestion and helps maintain stable blood sugar levels.

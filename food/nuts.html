Almonds

Description & Importance:

Almonds are a nutrient-dense food rich in healthy fats, protein, and antioxidants. They play an important role in heart health, blood sugar control, and providing sustained energy. Almonds are especially beneficial in balanced and weight-management diets when consumed in moderation.

Tips

Limit portion size (20–30 g per day)

Choose raw or dry-roasted almonds

Avoid salted or sugar-coated types




Nutrition Facts(per 100 g) vitamins
Calories 579 kcal E
Protein 21 g Calcium
Fat 50 g Potassium
Carbohydrates 22 g Magnesium

Wainuts

Description & Importance:

Walnuts are one of the best plant sources of omega-3 fatty acids. They are essential for brain health, heart protection, and reducing inflammation. Walnuts are highly beneficial for mental focus and cognitive function.

Tips

Eat a small handful daily

Best consumed raw

pistachios

Description & Importance:

Pistachios provide a good balance of protein, fiber, and healthy fats. They support heart health, digestion, and weight control due to their high satiety effect.

Tips

Choose unsalted pistachios

Control portions carefully

Ideal as a healthy snack

Cashews

Description & Importance:

Cashews are rich in healthy fats and essential minerals. They support bone health, energy production, and immune function. Despite being nutritious, they are calorie-dense and should be consumed in moderation.

Tips

Consume in small portions

Avoid flavored or fried cashews

Pair with fruit or yogurt

Calories 553 kcal B6
Protein 18 g Zinc
Fat 44 g Iron
Carbohydrates 30 g Magnesium

Healthy Food

Healthy food plays a vital role in maintaining both physical and mental well-being. It is not just about eating to satisfy hunger, but about nourishing the body with essential nutrients that help it function properly. A healthy diet provides the energy needed for daily activities, supports the immune system, improves concentration, and reduces the risk of chronic diseases. Eating healthy means consuming a balanced variety of foods that include fruits, vegetables, whole grains, lean proteins, and healthy fats. Fruits and vegetables are rich in vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants that protect the body from illness and strengthen immunity. Whole grains such as oats, brown rice, and whole wheat bread provide fiber, which aids digestion and helps maintain stable blood sugar levels.

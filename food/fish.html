White Fish (Sea bass / Sea bream – grilled)

Description & Importance:

White fish is a light, lean source of protein that is easy to digest and low in calories. It is ideal for people following weight-loss diets or those who want a healthy protein source without excess fat. White fish supports muscle maintenance while keeping the digestive system comfortable.

Tips

Best grilled or baked

Avoid frying

Season with lemon and herbs instead of butter




Nutrition Facts(per 100 g) vitamins
Calories 120 kcal B12
Protein 24 g B6
Fat 2 g Phosphorus
Carbohydrates 0 g Selenium

Salmon

Description & Importance:

Salmon is a fatty fish rich in healthy omega-3 fatty acids. It plays an important role in heart health, brain function, and inflammation reduction. Salmon is highly nutritious and suitable for balanced diets, even during weight loss when eaten in controlled portions.

Tips

Eat 1–2 times per week

Grill or bake for best results

Avoid creamy sauces




Nutrition Facts(per 100 g) vitamins
Calories 208 kcal B12
Protein 20 g Omega-3 fatty acids
Fat 13 g Selenium
Carbohydrates 0 g Potassium

Tuna

Description & Importance:

Tuna is a high-protein, low-fat fish commonly used in healthy meals. It helps meet daily protein needs and supports muscle recovery. Tuna is convenient, affordable, and effective for weight management.

Tips

Choose tuna in water, not oil

Limit to 2–3 times per week

Combine with vegetables, not mayonnaise




Nutrition Facts(per 100 g) vitamins
Calories 130 kcal B12
Protein 29 g B3
Fat 1 g Phosphorus
Carbohydrates 0 g Selenium

Healthy Food

Healthy food plays a vital role in maintaining both physical and mental well-being. It is not just about eating to satisfy hunger, but about nourishing the body with essential nutrients that help it function properly. A healthy diet provides the energy needed for daily activities, supports the immune system, improves concentration, and reduces the risk of chronic diseases. Eating healthy means consuming a balanced variety of foods that include fruits, vegetables, whole grains, lean proteins, and healthy fats. Fruits and vegetables are rich in vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants that protect the body from illness and strengthen immunity. Whole grains such as oats, brown rice, and whole wheat bread provide fiber, which aids digestion and helps maintain stable blood sugar levels.

Broccoli

Description & Importance:

Broccoli is a nutrient-dense vegetable that is very low in calories and high in fiber. It plays an important role in supporting the immune system and improving digestion. Broccoli is also rich in antioxidants, which help protect the body from chronic diseases.

Tips

Steam or lightly boil to preserve nutrients

Avoid overcooking

Combine with protein-rich meals




Nutrition Facts(per 100 g) vitamins
Calories 34 kcal C
Protein 2.8 g K
Fat 0.4 g A
Carbohydrates 7 g Folate

Zucchini

Description & Importance:

Zucchini is a light vegetable with high water content, making it ideal for hydration and weight loss. It supports digestion and adds volume to meals without adding many calories.

Tips

Best grilled or sautéed lightly

Can replace pasta in low-carb meals




Nutrition Facts(per 100 g) vitamins
Calories 17 kcal C
Protein 1.2 g B6
Fat 0.3 g Potassium
Carbohydrates 3 g Magnesium

Spinach

Description & Importance:

Spinach is a highly nutritious leafy green vegetable rich in iron and antioxidants. It supports blood health, energy production, and overall vitality.

Tips

Pair with vitamin C sources to enhance iron absorption

Healthy Food

Healthy food plays a vital role in maintaining both physical and mental well-being. It is not just about eating to satisfy hunger, but about nourishing the body with essential nutrients that help it function properly. A healthy diet provides the energy needed for daily activities, supports the immune system, improves concentration, and reduces the risk of chronic diseases. Eating healthy means consuming a balanced variety of foods that include fruits, vegetables, whole grains, lean proteins, and healthy fats. Fruits and vegetables are rich in vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants that protect the body from illness and strengthen immunity. Whole grains such as oats, brown rice, and whole wheat bread provide fiber, which aids digestion and helps maintain stable blood sugar levels.
